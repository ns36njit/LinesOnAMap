Central Asia

Central Asia is a region of asia comprising of the former soviet republics of Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Uzbekistan, Tajikistan, and Turkmenistan. This region has a rich history involving the Silk Road, Islam, and the Soviet Union. Central Asia is a little visited part of the world but many travelers would say that it is well worth the trip. When traveling this region it is highly beneficial to have some knowledge of the Russian language as it is still the language of inter-ethnic communication for many people, especially in Kazakhstan. The countries of Central Asia are relatively poor. Kazakhstan has similar economic development to Russia while the rest of the countries in Central Asia lag far behind. Due to this, many Central Asians seek employment abroad, mostly in Russia and sometimes in Turkey. Turkmenistan is very closed off and not recommended for travelers as it is very difficult to obtain a tourist visa. Central Asia as a whole has some of the most beautiful untouched nature in the world, a unique culinary experience unlike anywhere else in the world, and very hospitable people. Getting around Central Asia can be accomplished using trains and local busses.
